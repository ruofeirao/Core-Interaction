What is UV, and why is it important?

The UV Index is a numbered value that corresponds to the intensity of UV (ultraviolet) radiation from the sun. Although exposure to sunlight is often enjoyable, too much of it can be extremely harmful to your body.

When your skin is exposed to UV rays for an excessive amount of time, the DNA of your skin cells become damaged, and skin cancer can start when this damage is serious enough to affect skin cell growth.

No matter what your age or skin type is, it is important to always stay protected from overexposure to the sun since the sun's UV radiation poses serious risks of immediate visible damages such as sunburn, as well as the above-mentioned long-term effects of skin cancer.

Do not be tricked into thinking that you are safe from harm when the weather appears to be cloudy or rainy. Up to 80% of the sun's UV rays can still pass through clouds, and this is often the reason why many end up getting serious sunburns on 'harmless' overcast days if they've spent time outdoors with a lack of protection.
